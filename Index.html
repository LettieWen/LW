History of CAT Paving

Innovations for Better Infrastructure

1930’s

1933

Invention of the independent floating screed by Barber-Greene assured the success of the paver when used with the tamper bar

1934

Barber-Greene releases the world’s first production asphalt paver

1950's

1959

First use of hydraulics on a Barber-Greene paver making prevision and automated controls possible

1980’s

1980

The first hydraulically variable width screed, the Extend-A-Mat, is introduced by Barber-Greene

1984

Caterpillar signs a joint venture/branding agreement with CMI Corp

1985

CMI purchases RayGo and Albaret

1986

The first Cat branded paver, AP1200 with a Variable Increment Paving (VIP) screed, is released

1988

Joint venture between CMI and Caterpillar Inc. is dissolved Cat Paving Products Inc. is formed and acquires RayGo and Albert

1988

Dual pump propel system introduced on Vibratory Soil Compactors

1989

Exclusive pod-style drum with eccentric weight technology is introduced on compactors

1990’s

1991

Cat Paving Products Inc. acquires Barber-Greene

1996

Mobil-Trac™ undercarriage system introduced

1996

Most advanced material delivered system in the industry is integrated into pavers

2000’s

2000

Cat Paving Products Inc. acquires Bitelli to expand our global footprint

2001

Extensive research on soil and asphalt compaction technology begins

2004

Electric screed heat introduced eliminating the need for diesel fired heating system

2008

Tier 4/Stage IIIB engine technology introduced to reduce emissions and offer better fuel efficiency

2010’s

2012

B-Series Tandem Vibratory Rollers introduced with new operator controls

2014

Machine Drive Power (MDP) advanced soil compaction technology

2015

F-Series pavers introduced with an integrated generator, and exclusive screed heating system and advanced operator controls

2016

MDP and Compaction Meter Value (CMV) measurements are combined allowing the technology to work on all soil types

2016

Machine-to-machine communication of mapping information for asphalt compactors lays the foundation for future machine data sharing and autonomous control

2016

Introduction of the new generation of reinvented Cold Planers, designed for ease of use and serviceability

2017

Paver Thermal Scanning is introduced to help detect thermal variation caused by the delivery process
